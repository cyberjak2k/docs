## 0.Precautions

!!! warning "warning"
    When fastening the printed parts with screws, do not tighten them too much to avoid damaging the printed parts.


!!! info "info"
    Section of the printout needs to be pressed into heat insert using a soldering iron:
    ![image.webp](1.webp){.img1}

## 1.Limit assembly

### Required materials

1.Photoelectric limit switch * 3
   ![image.webp](xwkg.webp){.img1}

2.M3*8 countersunk screws * 12
   ![image.webp](M3X8.webp){.img1}

3.T-Nut type 20 - M3 * 12
   ![image.webp](20M3.webp){.img1}

### Printed parts

1.[Limiting and fixing component] * 3
[Limiting and fixing component]: dy1.stl
   ![image.webp](dy1.webp){.img1}

### Assembly process

1. Insert the <strong>photoelectric limiting</strong> into the <strong>limiting fixing component</strong>, and and secure it with two <strong>M3*8 countersunk screws</strong>, as shown in the figure.
   ![image.webp](2.webp){.img1}

!!! info "info"
    Do not overtighten to avoid damaging the printed part.

2.Pull the <strong>connecting wire</strong> out from the right side of the <strong>limiting and fixing component</strong> to prevent the stepper motor from pressing on the wire during installation.
   ![image.webp](3.webp){.img1}

3.Use two <strong>M3*8 countersunk screws</strong> to pass through the <strong>limiting fastener</strong> and screw on two drop-in t-nuts type 20-M3, as shown in the picture.
   ![image.webp](4.webp){.img1}

!!! info "info"
    Do not tighten them completely, just tighten them.

4.Secure the <strong>limiting fastener</strong> to the frame, during installation, ensure the protrusion of the <strong>metal connecting slider</strong> is centered on the photoelectric sensor of the <strong>photoelectric limiting switch</strong>.
   ![image.webp](5.webp){.img1}

5.Secure to the <strong>short aluminum profile</strong>, pay attention to the position of the 20-type M3 boat-shaped nut, it should be properly seated within the groove of the short profile. 
   ![image.webp](6.webp){.img1}

!!! info "info"
    Do not overtighten to avoid damaging the printed parts.

6.The installation methods for the other two groups are the same.
   ![image.webp](7.webp){.img1}

## 3.Left side of the skirt hem

### Required materials

1.M3*8 countersunk screws * 6
   ![image.webp](M3X8.webp){.img1}

2.Heat inserts * 3
   ![image.webp](tlm.webp){.img1}
   
3.T-Nut type 20 - M3 * 6
   ![image.webp](20M3.webp){.img1}

### Printed parts

1.[Left skirt hem printout]  * 3
[Left skirt hem printout]: dy3.stl
   ![image.webp](dy3.webp){.img1}

### Assembly process

1.Use a soldering iron to press the <strong>heat insert</strong> into the printed part on the <strong>left skirt</strong>.
   ![image.webp](12.webp){.img1}

2.Pass the <strong>M3*8 countersunk screw</strong> through the printed part on the <strong>left skirt</strong> and fit the <strong>drop-in T-nuts,type 20-M3</strong>.
   ![image.webp](13.webp){.img1}

3.Simply screw on the <strong>M3*8 countersunk screws</strong>; no need to tighten them completely.
   ![image.webp](14.webp){.img1}

4.Secure it to the <strong>short aluminum profile</strong>, pay attention to the position of the drop-in T-nuts,type 20-M3; it should be properly seated within the groove of the <strong>short profile</strong>. Do not overtighten to avoid damaging the printed parts. 
   ![image.webp](20.webp){.img1}

5.The installation methods for the other two groups are the same.
   ![image.webp](21.webp){.img1}
