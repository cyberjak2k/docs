## 1.Guide rail assembly

### Required materials

1.Linear guide rail * 3
   ![image.webp](dg.webp){.img1}

2.M3*10 cap head screws * 24
   ![image.webp](M3-10.webp){.img1}

3.M3*14 cap head screws * 3
   ![image.webp](M3X14.webp){.img1}

4.T-Nut type 30 - M3 * 27
   ![image.webp](30M3.webp){.img1}

### Printed parts

1.[Linear rail guide] * 2
[Linear rail guide]: dy1.stl
   ![image.webp](dy1.webp){.img1}

2.[Linear rail stop] * 3
[Linear rail stop]: dy2.stl
   ![image.webp](dy2.webp){.img1}

### Assembly process

1. Take out the linear rail and use two linear rail guides to support it, use M3*10 cap-head screw to pass through the guide rail, installing one screw every two holes from top to bottom, for a total of eight screws. 
   ![image.webp](1.webp){.img1}

!!! info "info"
    It is recommended to do this on a clean desktop.

2. Insert the linear rail stop into the linear rail using a M3*14 cap-head screw.
   ![image.webp](2.webp){.img1}

3. Lightly tighten the nine drop-in t-nuts, type 20-M3, but do not tighten them completely.
   ![image.webp](3.webp){.img1}

4. Place the linear rail on to the long profile. 
   ![image.webp](4.webp){.img1}

!!! info "info"
    The t-nuts type 20-M3 should be in the groove. 

    The guide linear rail stop is fixed at the bottom, as shown in the figure.

    Use two linear rail guides to assist in the left and right positioning of the profile to prevent the slider from falling off. 

5. Tighten the M3*10 cap-head screw，ensuring that all t-nuts type 20-M3 are fully engaged in the long profile groove. 

   ![image.webp](5.webp){.img1}

!!! info "info"
    After installation, slide the slider on the linear rail to check for smooth movement without jamming. 
    
    Add lubricant as needed to ensure smooth slider movement. Finally, remove the Linear rail guides, as shown in the figure. 

6. The installation method for the other two is the same.
   ![image.webp](10.webp){.img1}

### Assembly process

1. Thread the 2GT open-end timing belt through to the two stepper motors，and fit both ends into the grooves of the stepper motor timing pulleys as shown in the figure.
   ![image.webp](6.webp){.img1}

2. Wrap both of the ends of the 2GT open-end timing belt around the metal connecting slider, and secure them with Cable ties. Then trim off any excess cable tie, as shown in the figure.
   ![image.webp](7.webp){.img1}

3. Use 2 M3*20 cap-head screws to pass through the slider washer and connect to the metal slider then secure it to the slider of the linear guide rail. Finally, tighten them as shown in the figure.
   ![image.webp](8.webp){.img1}
   ![image.webp](9.webp){.img1}

4. Use two M3*14 cap-head screws to pass through the s lider washer and connect them to the metal slider, after applying threadlocker secure them to the slider of the linear guide rail, and tighten them as shown in the figure. 
   ![image.webp](11.webp){.img1}

5. Attach the decorative slider block to the metal connecting slider，as shown in the image.
   ![image.webp](12.webp){.img1}
   ![image.webp](13.webp){.img1}

6. The installation methods for the other two groups are the same.

## 3.Transmission Adjustment

### Required materials

1.Torsion springs * 3
   ![image.webp](th.webp){.img1}

2.Cable ties * 3
   ![image.webp](zd.webp){.img1}

### Printed parts

1.[Belt tension adjuster] * 3
[Belt tension adjuster]: dy5.stl
   ![image.webp](dy5.webp){.img1}

### Assembly process

1. Connect the belt tension adjuster to the 2GT open timing belt as shown in the figure.
   ![image.webp](15.webp){.img1}

2. Use cable ties to tighten the belt tensioner to make the 2GT open timing belt taut.
   ![image.webp](16.webp){.img1}
   
3. Slide the metal connecting slider up and down to observe whether the edge of the timing pulley gets stuck during the movement of the 2GT open timing belt as shown in the figure.
   ![image.webp](17.webp){.img1}
   ![image.webp](18.webp){.img1}

4. The tightness of the two parallel screws above and below the stepper motor can be adjusted to change the stepper motor angle, ensuring that the 2GT open timing belt does not rub against the edge of the stepper motor's timing pulley during operation.
   ![image.webp](19.webp){.img1}
   ![image.webp](zq.webp){.img1}

5. Finally, install the torsion spring at the position shown in the figure to provide a certain self-preload.
   ![image.webp](20.webp){.img1}

???- faq "FAQ"

    **Q: What is the function of a shock absorber pad?**

    **A:** The stepper motor and metal foot pads are made of metal, and the vibration generated by the high-speed movement of the motor will be transmitted to the frame, and the use of flexible gaskets can effectively isolate vibration and noise, and because it is a flexible gasket, it is very convenient to adjust the angle of the motor by adjusting the tightness of the screws, further reducing the difficulty of assembly and debugging.
