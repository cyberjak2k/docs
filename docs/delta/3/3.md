## 1.Guide rail assembly

### Required materials

1.Linear guide rail * 3
   ![image.webp](dg.webp){.img1}

2.M3*10 cup head screws * 24
   ![image.webp](M3-10.webp){.img1}

3.M3*14 cup head screws * 3
   ![image.webp](M3X14.webp){.img1}

4.T-Nut type 30 - M3 * 27
   ![image.webp](30M3.webp){.img1}

### Printed parts

1.[Guide rail positioning tool] * 2
[Guide rail positioning tool]: dy1.stl
   ![image.webp](dy1.webp){.img1}

2.[Guide rail anti-detachment block] * 3
[Guide rail anti-detachment block]: dy2.stl
   ![image.webp](dy2.webp){.img1}

### Assembly process

1.Take out the <strong>linear guide rail </strong>and use two<strong> guide rail positioning tools</strong> to support it, use <strong>M3*10 cup-head screw</strong> to pass through the guide rail, installing one screw every two holes from top to bottom, for a total of eight screws. 
   ![image.webp](1.webp){.img1}

!!! info "info"
    It is recommended to do this on a clean desktop.

2.Insert the <strong>guide rail anti-detachment block</strong> into the linear rail using a <strong>M3*14 cup-head screw</strong>.
   ![image.webp](2.webp){.img1}

3.Tighten the nine drop-in t-nuts, type 20-M3, but do not tighten them completely.
   ![image.webp](3.webp){.img1}

4.Place the <strong>linear guide rail</strong> on to the <strong>long profile</strong>. 
   ![image.webp](4.webp){.img1}

!!! info "info"
    The drop-in t-nuts type 20-M3 should be in the groove. 

    The <strong>guide rail anti-detachment block</strong> is fixed at the bottom, as shown in the figure.

    Use two <strong>Guide Rail Positioning tools</strong> to assist in the left and right positioning of the profile to prevent the slider from falling off. 

5.Tighten the <strong>M3*10 cup-head screw</strong>，ensuring that all <strong>drop-in t-nuts type 20-M3</strong> are fully engaged in the <strong>long profile </strong> groove. 

   ![image.webp](5.webp){.img1}

!!! info "info"
    After installation, slide the slider on the linear guide rail to check for smooth movement without jamming. 
    
    Add lubricant as needed to ensure smooth slider movement. Finally, remove the guide rail positioning tool, as shown in the figure. 

6.The installation method for the other two is the same.
   ![image.webp](10.webp){.img1}
   
## 2.Slider assembly

### Required materials

1.Metal connecting slider * 3
   ![image.webp](hk.webp){.img1}

2.Metal ball joint connector * 3
   ![image.webp](ljj.webp){.img1}

3.2GT Open-End Synchronous Belt * 3
   ![image.webp](pd.webp){.img1}

4.Cable ties * 12
   ![image.webp](zd.webp){.img1}

5.M3*14 cup head screws * 6
   ![image.webp](M3X14.webp){.img1}

6.M3*20 cup head screws * 6
   ![image.webp](M3X20.webp){.img1}


### Printed parts

1.[Slider pads] * 3
[Slider pads]: dy3.stl
   ![image.webp](dy3.webp){.img1}

2.[Slider decorative block] * 3
[Slider decorative block]: dy4.stl
   ![image.webp](dy4.webp){.img1}

### Assembly process

1.Thread the <strong>2GT open-end timing belt</strong> through to the two <strong>stepper motors</strong>，and fit both ends into the grooves of the stepper motor <strong>timing pulleys</strong> as shown in the figure.
   ![image.webp](6.webp){.img1}

2.Wrap both of the ends of the <strong>2GT open-end timing belt </strong>around the <strong>metal connecting slider </strong>, and secure them with <strong>Cable ties </strong>. Then trim off any excess cable tie, as shown in the figure.
   ![image.webp](7.webp){.img1}

3.Use 2 <strong>M3*20 cup-head screws </strong>to pass through the <strong>slider washer </strong> and connect to the <strong>metal slider</strong> then fix it to the slider of the <strong> linear guide rail </strong> finally, tighten them as shown in the figure.
   ![image.webp](8.webp){.img1}
   ![image.webp](9.webp){.img1}

4.Use two <strong>M3*14 cup-head screws </strong>to pass through the <strong>slider washer</strong> and connect them to the <strong>metal slider</strong>, after applying <strong>threadlocker</strong> fix them to the slider of the <strong>linear guide rail</strong>, and tighten them as shown in the figure. 
   ![image.webp](11.webp){.img1}

5.Attach the decorative slider block the metal connecting slider，as shown in the image.
   ![image.webp](12.webp){.img1}
   ![image.webp](13.webp){.img1}

6.The installation methods for the other two groups are the same.
   ![image.webp](14.webp){.img1}

  
## 3.Transmission Adjustment

### Required materials

1.Torsion springs * 3
   ![image.webp](th.webp){.img1}

2.Cable ties * 3
   ![image.webp](zd.webp){.img1}

### Printed parts

1.[Belt tension adjuster] * 3
[Belt tension adjuster]: dy5.stl
   ![image.webp](dy5.webp){.img1}

### Assembly process

1.Connect the <strong>belt tension adjuster</strong> to the <strong>2GT open timing belt</strong> as shown in the figure.
   ![image.webp](15.webp){.img1}

2.Use <strong>cable ties </strong> to tighten the <strong>belt tensioner</strong> to make the <strong>2GT open timing belt</strong> taut.
   ![image.webp](16.webp){.img1}
   
3.Slide the metal connecting slider up and down to observe whether the edge of the timing pulley gets stuck during the movement of the <strong>2GT open timing belt</strong> as shown in the figure.
   ![image.webp](17.webp){.img1}
   ![image.webp](18.webp){.img1}

4.The tightness of the two parallel screws above and below the stepper motor can be adjusted to change the stepper motor angle, ensuring that the <strong>2GT open timing belt</strong> does not rub against the edge of the <strong>stepper motor's timing pulley</strong> during operation.
   ![image.webp](19.webp){.img1}
   ![image.webp](zq.webp){.img1}

5.Finally, install the <strong>torsion spring</strong> at the position shown in the figure to provide a certain self-preload.
   ![image.webp](20.webp){.img1}

   
???- tip "Another method for installing belts"

    This is another installation method for the <strong>2GT open timing belt</strong>, which was the original design. 

    It replaces the original timing belt with a thicker and stronger specification, supporting a maximum width of <strong>10mm</strong>.

    ![image.webp](kz1.webp){.img1}
    ![image.webp](kz2.webp){.img1}

    Using another type of MGN slider allows for direct fixation without the need for <strong>slider washers</strong>. 
    
    Due to changes in the mass-produced linear guide, a printed part was created for lifting to avoid adding additional screw types.（Note: If using screws directly instead of a printed part, <strong>M3*20 cup-head screws</strong> require the application of thread-locking adhesive for a tighter hold.)
