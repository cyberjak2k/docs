## 0.Precautions

!!! warning "warning"
    When fastening the printed parts with screws，do not tighten them too much to avoid damaging the printed parts.


!!! info "info"
    Section of the printout needs to be pressed into heat insert using a soldering iron:

## 1.Lower decorative piece

### Required materials

1.Foot pads * 3
   ![image.webp](jd.webp){.img1}

2.T-Nut type 20 - M3 * 6
   ![image.webp](20M3.webp){.img1}

3.M3*10 cup head screw * 3
   ![image.webp](M5X16.webp){.img1}

4.M3*10 cup head screw * 6
   ![image.webp](M3X10.webp){.img1}



### Printed parts

1.[Undercut Decoration] * 3
[Undercut Decoration]: dy1.stl
   ![image.webp](dy1.webp){.img1}

### Assembly process

1.Screw the M5*16 cup-head screw through the feet and into the the decorative part of the lower bracket.
   ![image.webp](2.webp){.img1}

2.Screw the M3*10 cup-head screw into the decorative piece of the lower part and fit the drop-in t-nut type 20-M3.
   ![image.webp](3.webp){.img1}

3.Simply screw on the M3*10 cup head screw ; no need to tighten it completely.
   ![image.webp](4.webp){.img1}

4.Secure to the short aluminum profile.
   ![image.webp](5.webp){.img1}

5.Pay attention to the position ofthe drop-in nut, type 20-M3.It should be secured to the profile, but do not overtighten it to avoid damaging the lower decorative parts.
   ![image.webp](6.webp){.img1}

6.The installation methods for the other two groups are the same.
   ![image.webp](7.webp){.img1}

### Printed parts

### Assembly process

1.Use a soldering iron to press the heat insert into the screen mounting bracket.
   ![image.webp](29.webp){.img1}

2.Pass the M3*8 countersunk screw through the screen mounting bracket and fit the drop-in t-nut, type 20 - M3.
   ![image.webp](30.webp){.img1}

3.Simply screw in the M3*8 countersunk screws; no need to tighten them completely.
   ![image.webp](31.webp){.img1}

4.Install the touchscreen into the screen mounting bracket as shown in the figure.
   ![image.webp](32.webp){.img1}
      

5.Press the Screen Front Bezel onto the screen, being careful not to use too much force to avoid damaging the screen. 
   ![image.webp](33.webp){.img1}
   
6.When correctly inserted，the front bezel of the screenshould engage with the clips on the screen mounting bracket.
   ![image.webp](34.webp){.img1}

7.Secure to the short aluminum profile.
   ![image.webp](35.webp){.img1}


## 7.Electrical fixtures

### Required materials

1.Bottom fixing plate * 1
   ![image.webp](36.webp){.img1}

2.Solid State Relay * 1
   ![image.webp](jdq.webp){.img1}

3.Switching power supply * 1
   ![image.webp](dy.webp){.img1}
   
4.M3*8 countersunk screws * 18
   ![image.webp](M3X8.webp){.img1}

5.M3*10 cup head screws * 2
   ![image.webp](M3X10.webp){.img1}

6.M3 self-locking nuts * 2
   ![image.webp](zslm.webp){.img1}

### Assembly process

1.Place the the switching power supplyon thebottom mounting plate, as shown in the figure.
   ![image.webp](37.webp){.img1}
   ![image.webp](38.webp){.img1}

2.Turn over and secure the the switching power supply with four M3*8 countersunk screws, as shown in the picture.
   ![image.webp](39.webp){.img1}

3.Place the solid-state relay onto the bottom mounting plate, as shown in the figure.
   ![image.webp](40.webp){.img1}

4.Note the markings on the solid-state relay, as shown in the figure.
   ![image.webp](41.webp){.img1}

5.Use two M3*10 cup-head screws to pass through the screw holes on the solid-state relay.
   ![image.webp](42.webp){.img1}
      
6.Turn over and secure with two M3 self-locking nuts.
   ![image.webp](43.webp){.img1}
   
7.Secure to the bottom using twelveM3*8 countersunk screws.
   ![image.webp](44.webp){.img1}

8.After completion, turn the printer over. Note thattouchscreenshould be facing forward, and the power switch and the solid-state relayshould be on the front right side.
   ![image.webp](45.webp){.img1}

???- tip "Reserved for expansion"
    The four pre-drilled holes at the bottom, designated for expansion, allow for the installation of a second high-voltage power supply. These holes are compatible with both MW and MORNSUN screw holes, and the installation method is the same.
    ![image.webp](46.webp){.img1}
