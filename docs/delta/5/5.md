## 0.Precautions

!!! warning "warning"
    When fastening the printed parts with screws, do not tighten them too much to avoid damaging the printed parts.
    
    Screws that need to be coated with threadlocker, will show in <strong><span style="color:#295FCC">Blue</span></strong>：
    ![image.webp](ls1.webp){.img1}
    
!!! info "info"
    Section of the printout needs to be pressed into heat insert using a soldering iron:
    ![image.webp](qrlm.webp){.img1}

???+ tip "tip"
    When securing the <strong>self-locking nut</strong>, it is recommended to use a wrench for assistance:
    ![image.webp](bs.webp){.img1}
    For unthreaded printed parts, using a power tool will make installation easier:
    ![image.webp](ddgj.webp){.img1}

   ![image.webp](dt.webp){.img1}

   ![image.webp](M3X10.webp){.img1}

7.Self-locking nuts * 6
   ![image.webp](zslm.webp){.img1}

### Assembly process

1.Insert two <strong>M3*10 cup-head screws</strong> into the <strong>metal ball joint connector</strong>, as shown in the figure.
   ![image.webp](1.webp){.img1}

2.Apply <strong>thread-locking adhesive</strong> to the <strong>M3*10 cup-head screws</strong>, insert them into the <strong>metal mounting bracket</strong>, and tighten using two <strong>self-locking nuts</strong>.
   ![image.webp](ls.webp){.img1}
   ![image.webp](2.webp){.img1}

3.Please ensure it is tightened securely, a wrench can be used for additional tightening.
   ![image.webp](3.webp){.img1}
   ![image.webp](4.webp){.img1}

4.The installation methods for the other two groups are the same.
   ![image.webp](5.webp){.img1}

5.Apply <strong>thread-locking adhesive</strong> to the four <strong>M2.5*6 cup-head screws</strong>.
   ![image.webp](ls1.webp){.img1}
   
6.Cut a <strong>14MM</strong> section of <strong>PTFE tubing</strong>.
   ![image.webp](6.webp){.img1}

7.Using the short tube as a positioning reference, connect the <strong>printhead assembly</strong> to the <strong>metal platform</strong> as shown in the figure.
   ![image.webp](7.webp){.img1}
   
8.Secure the <strong>printhead assembly</strong> to the <strong>metal mounting platen (Effector)</strong> using four <strong>M2.5*6 cup-head screws</strong>, tightening them securely.
   ![image.webp](8.webp){.img1}

9.Pass the <strong>printhead assembly</strong> wires through the <strong>metal hanger (Effector)</strong>, as shown in the diagram.
   ![image.webp](9.webp){.img1}

### Assembly process

1.Press the <strong>MR85 bearing</strong> into the <strong>extruder back plate</strong>, as shown in the figure.
   ![image.webp](10.webp){.img1}
   ![image.webp](11.webp){.img1}


2.Insert the </strong>main filament gear</strong> into the <strong>POM gear</strong>, paying attention to the direction of the main filament gear's opening, as shown in the figure.
   ![image.webp](12.webp){.img1}
   ![image.webp](13.webp){.img1}

3.After applying <strong>threadlocker</strong> to the <strong>grub screw</strong>, tighten it, the set screw should be touhing the flat side of the <strong>POM gear</strong> shaft, as shown in the figure.
   ![image.webp](14.webp){.img1}
   ![image.webp](15.webp){.img1}
   ![image.webp](16.webp){.img1}

4.Insert the </strong>MR85 bearing</strong> onto the <strong>POM gear</strong> shaft, as shown in the figure.
   ![image.webp](17.webp){.img1}

5.Use a soldering iron to press the <strong>heat insert</strong> into the <strong>extruder printout</strong>, as shown in the picture.
   ![image.webp](18.webp){.img1}
   ![image.webp](19.webp){.img1}

6.Connect the <strong>POM gear</strong> to the <strong>extruder printout</strong>, the <strong>MR85 bearing</strong> should be secured in the slot of the <strong>extruder printout</strong>, as shown in the figure.
   ![image.webp](20.webp){.img1}
   ![image.webp](21.webp){.img1}

7.Connect the <strong>POM gear</strong> to the <strong>extruder back plate</strong>, the <strong>POM gear</strong> should be inserted into the <strong>MR85 bearing</strong> of the <strong>extruder back plate</strong>, as shown in the figure.
   ![image.webp](22.webp){.img1}
   ![image.webp](23.webp){.img1}

8.Insert two <strong>nylon spacer posts</strong> into the two holes below the <strong>extruder back plate</strong> and the <strong>extruder printout</strong>, pass two <strong>M3 *20 cup-head screws</strong> through them and tighten the <strong>self-locking nuts</strong>, as shown in the picture.
   ![image.webp](24.webp){.img1}
   ![image.webp](25.webp){.img1}
   ![image.webp](26.webp){.img1}
   ![image.webp](27.webp){.img1}

9.Insert two <strong>nylon spacers</strong> into the two holes above the <strong>extruder back plate</strong> and the <strong>extruder printout</strong>, use two <strong>M3 *35 cup-head screws</strong> to pass them through and screw them onto the <strong>extruder motor</strong>, pass them through the bellows <strong>fixing piece</strong> and tighten the <strong>self - locking nuts</strong>, as shown in the figure.
   ![image.webp](28.webp){.img1}
   ![image.webp](29.webp){.img1}
   ![image.webp](30.webp){.img1}
   ![image.webp](31.webp){.img1}
   ![image.webp](32.webp){.img1}
   ![image.webp](33.webp){.img1}

10.After inserting the <strong>two needle roller bearings into the <strong>short extruder shaft</strong> , insert them into the </strong> Extruder idler gear</strong> and press them into the <strong>extruder idler part</strong>, as shown in the figure. 

!!! warning "warning"
    Note the direction in which the <strong>extruder idler gear</strong> is pressed in.
   ![image.webp](34.webp){.img1}
   ![image.webp](35.webp){.img1}
   ![image.webp](36.webp){.img1}
   ![image.webp](37.webp){.img1}
   ![image.webp](38.webp){.img1}
   ![image.webp](39.webp){.img1}

!!! info "info"
    It swould be recomended to lubricate the bearings with tiny amount a ptfe grease, as well as where the teeth meet the main filament gear.

11.Connect the </strong> long extruder shaft</strong> through the <strong>extruder printed part</strong> and the <strong>extruder idler part</strong>, as shown in the figure.
   ![image.webp](40.webp){.img1}
   ![image.webp](41.webp){.img1}

!!! info "Pressing the extruder shaft"
       If the <strong>long extruder shaft</strong> is difficult to press in, a power tool can be used to clamp the <strong>long extruder shaft</strong> to enlarge the hole in the <strong>extruder print</strong>. 

       Do not press it in forcefully to avoid damaging the <strong>extruder print</strong>.


12.Pass two <strong>M3*14 cup head screws</strong> through the <strong>extruder</strong>, and connect it to the <strong>metal mounting platform ( effector )</strong>, then tighten two <strong>self-locking nuts</strong>, as shown in the figure.
   ![image.webp](42.webp){.img1}
   ![image.webp](43.webp){.img1}
   ![image.webp](44.webp){.img1}

13.Insert the <strong>tension thumb screw</strong> through the <strong>spring and washer</strong>, and screw it onto the <strong>heat insert </strong> of the <strong>extruder print</strong>, as shown in the figure.
   ![image.webp](45.webp){.img1}
   ![image.webp](46.webp){.img1}
   ![image.webp](47.webp){.img1}
   ![image.webp](48.webp){.img1}
